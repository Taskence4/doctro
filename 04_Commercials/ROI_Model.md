### Revenue Projection for Doctor

**Assumptions:**
- Doctor currently has 0–5 Google reviews and weak or no GBP presence
- Clinic runs 15–25 consultations/day, 6 days/week
- Average consultation fee: ₹500–₹2,000 (varies by specialty)
- Conservative assumption: 1–2 new patients/week from digital discovery in month 3+ [ASSUMPTION]

| Scenario | New Patients/Week | Monthly Revenue Added | Annual Revenue Added | Annual ROI |
|----------|:-----------------:|:--------------------:|:-------------------:|:----------:|
| Conservative | 2 | ₹5,200 | ₹62,400 | –67% (payback in 36 mo) |
| Moderate | 5 | ₹13,000 | ₹1,56,000 | –17% (payback in 14 mo) |
| Strong | 10 | ₹26,000 | ₹3,12,000 | +66% net return |
| High-value specialist | 4 × ₹2,500 avg | ₹40,000 | ₹4,80,000 | +155% net return |

*Conservative scenario uses ₹500 avg consult; high-value uses ₹2,500 (specialist). Most referral doctors sit in the moderate–strong range.*

**Break-even:** At moderate scenario, subscription pays back in ~14 months. At strong, in under 7 months.

### Beyond Direct Consultation Revenue

The bigger ROI driver for many doctors isn't consultation fees — it's:

- **Reduced walk-in loss:** Patients who Google and don't find you go elsewhere. Fixing this alone recovers ₹1–3L/year in missed appointments
- **Stronger Practo + directory presence:** Increased inbound appointment bookings without referral dependence
- **Review reputation:** A doctor going from 3 to 30+ reviews sees 40–60% more profile clicks [ASSUMPTION based on GBP industry data]
- **Referral network credibility:** A specialist with 50+ reviews and an active digital profile gets more peer-to-peer referrals from other doctors

### Intangible Benefits

- Professional credibility and trust signals before the first patient visit
- Reduced dependence on word-of-mouth alone
- Review base for insurance empanelment and hospital panel applications
- Authority positioning vs. less visible competitors in the same specialty + area
