## SECTION 6: SENSITIVITY ANALYSIS

### What if churn is higher than expected?

| Monthly Churn Rate | Doctors at Month 12 | Monthly Revenue (Month 12) | Impact |
|:-----------------:|:-------------------:|:--------------------------:|--------|
| 3% | 96 | ₹14.7L | Minimal |
| 7% | 88 | ₹13.5L | Manageable |
| 12% | 75 | ₹11.5L | Significant — requires retention program |
| 20% | 55 | ₹8.5L | Critical — near break-even territory |

> Retention is the most important economic lever. A 1% improvement in monthly retention = ~₹10L+ in LTV across 100 doctors.

### What if only 40% T2 (not 65%) — heavier T1 mix?

| Tier Mix | Blended GP/Doctor | 100-Doc Monthly Profit |
|----------|:-----------------:|:----------------------:|
| 20/65/15 (target) | ₹12,799 | ₹6,09,370 |
| 40/40/20 (T1 heavy) | ₹10,934 | ₹4,26,900 |
| 10/80/10 (T2 dominant) | ₹12,960 | ₹6,25,500 |

> The economics hold across all reasonable mixes. The key risk is a T1-heavy portfolio — push toward T2 actively.
