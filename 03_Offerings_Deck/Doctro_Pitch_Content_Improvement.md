This document is the content strategy and rewrite guide for [Doctro_Pitch_Reveal.html](/c:/AI-Lab/projects/referral%20doctors%20digital%20presence%20build/03_Offerings_Deck/Doctro_Pitch_Reveal.html).

## Brand Tone

### Brand role

`Doctro` is a specialist visibility partner for referral-led clinics.

It helps clinics make their doctors easier to discover, easier to trust, and easier to contact across the digital touchpoints patients already use.

### Primary audience

Clinic decision-makers:
- clinic founders
- practice owners
- administrators
- growth-minded specialty clinic operators

### Tone attributes

- Professional
- Calm
- Credible
- Specialist-first
- Commercially clear
- Modern, but not trendy

### How Doctro should sound

- Like a trusted operating partner, not a promotional agency
- Like a clinic-side advisor who understands patient trust
- Like a premium service that improves public presence with discipline and consistency

### How Doctro should not sound

- Hype-heavy
- Startup-pitchy
- Automation-first
- Agency-generic
- Overly creative or brand-theory heavy
- Overloaded with `AI` language

### Tone formula

Use:
- clear commercial language
- patient-trust framing
- specialist credibility language
- practical outcome language

Avoid:
- exaggerated promises
- inflated metrics without evidence
- jargon like `growth hacks`, `funnels`, `lead-gen engine`, `omnichannel dominance`
- repeated `AI-powered` claims unless operationally necessary

## Positioning And Message Hierarchy

### One-line positioning

`Doctro is a done-for-you specialist visibility partner that helps referral-led clinics present their doctors with more clarity, credibility, and consistency online.`

### Core problem statement

Many specialists are clinically trusted offline but under-presented online. Patients now validate doctors digitally before they call or visit, and a weak public profile can quietly erode confidence.

### Value proposition

Doctro helps clinics turn a fragmented doctor footprint into a polished visibility system across Google, profile pages, reviews, social presence, and patient contact assets.

### Proof pillars

1. `Discoverability`
Doctors become easier to find across search, Maps, and profile destinations.

2. `Trust`
Patients see clearer credentials, stronger profiles, and better public proof.

3. `Professional presence`
The doctor appears current, established, and well represented.

4. `Inquiry readiness`
Patients get cleaner pathways to call, message, locate, and book.

5. `Repeatable support`
The clinic gets ongoing monthly visibility support instead of a one-time setup.

### Message hierarchy

The deck should communicate in this order:

1. Patient behavior has changed.
2. Specialists are being judged digitally before consultation.
3. Many clinics are under-representing good doctors online.
4. Doctro solves that with a structured visibility service.
5. The output is visible, practical, and commercially useful.

## Approved Vocabulary

### Preferred phrases

- specialist visibility
- digital presence
- patient trust
- professional presence
- referral-led clinic
- public-facing profile
- visibility stack
- trust assets
- clinic-ready
- done-for-you support
- monthly visibility support
- easier to discover, trust, and contact

### Phrases to reduce or avoid

- AI-powered growth engine
- automation-led marketing
- digital domination
- lead generation machine
- social media management for doctors
- branding agency for clinics
- growth hacking
- performance marketing stack

### Word replacement guide

Replace:
- `digital growth` -> `specialist visibility`
- `marketing` -> `public presence` or `visibility support`
- `brand execution` -> `doctor-facing assets` or `patient-facing collateral`
- `delivery` -> `visible outputs` or `profile system`
- `conversion` -> `inquiry and booking intent`
- `AI-powered` -> remove unless operationally necessary

## Content Guardrails

- Do not use unsupported percentages or benchmarks unless sourced externally and approved.
- Do not overclaim appointments, revenue, or patient outcomes.
- Do not frame the service as a generic agency retainership.
- Do not mention internal automation workflows, tool stack, or operational infrastructure in the client deck.
- Do not use hospital-network sponsorship framing unless the deck is intentionally tailored for that audience.
- Keep the tone advisory and premium, especially in commercial slides.
- Preserve a distinction between:
  - what Doctro does
  - what gets built
  - what outcomes the clinic can reasonably expect

## Slide Update Checklist For The HTML Rewrite Phase

- Update every slide kicker to match the revised hierarchy.
- Remove unsourced statistics from the current `Why now` slide.
- Rename `Brand Execution` to `Patient-Facing Assets`.
- Rename `Digital Delivery` to `Core Presence System`.
- Tighten commercial slide language so packages feel advisory, not promotional.
- Keep `Doctro` consistent across title, footer, and contact references.
- Keep `AI`, `automation`, and backend execution details out of visible copy.

## Final Writing Standard

Every slide should pass this filter:

- Does this sound like a clinic-side visibility partner?
- Does this reduce rather than increase jargon?
- Does this make the doctor more credible, not more marketed?
- Does this tell the clinic what they get and why it matters?
- Would a decision-maker understand the offer in under 20 seconds?
